Privacy & Safety

Privacy (App & data)

  • Local-first design: meal logs and profiles are stored on your device by default. Optional sync may be offered and is off unless you enable it.
  • Minimal network use: used for device provisioning, optional updates, and features you explicitly start.
  • No medical use: nutrition info is educational only and not a diagnosis.
  • Clear permissions: camera/photos (meal photos), Wi-Fi (provisioning), storage (saving data).
  • Data control: you can delete a child profile or clear local data from the app settings.
  • Contact: questions about data? hello@bitebuddy.app

Safety (Hardware use)

  • No submersion: the electronics are not waterproof. Keep ports dry and do not dishwash the electronics unit.
  • Cleaning: power off, then wipe with a soft damp cloth and mild dish soap; dry fully before use. Avoid solvents/abrasives.
  • Charging: use a standard 5V USB-C charger. Fast-charge modes above device specs are not recommended.
  • Cables around kids: route charging cables out of reach; supervise young children.
  • Heat & damage: stop using and unplug if you notice swelling, odor, or unusual heat.

Information above describes intended behavior. Details can vary by firmware/app version and model.

FAQ

Allergies — can the plate detect allergens?
The plate does not detect allergens. In the app you can tag foods with common allergens and see reminders, but always check product labels and follow your clinician’s guidance for severe allergies.
Waterproofing — is it water-resistant?
The electronics unit is not designed for immersion or high-pressure water. Keep ports dry and avoid splashes. If your model includes a removable food tray, follow the label on that tray for washing instructions.
Cleaning — how should I clean it?
1) Power off and unplug. 2) Wipe with a soft cloth dampened with warm water and a small amount of mild dish soap. 3) Wipe again with clean water. 4) Dry completely. Avoid bleach, alcohol soaking, or abrasive pads, and never spray water into the ports.
Dishwasher — can I put it in?
Do not put the electronics unit in a dishwasher. If your model has a detachable food-contact tray labeled as dishwasher-safe, place only that tray on the top rack. Check your unit’s label for confirmation.
Charging — how do I charge it and how long does it take?
Use a standard 5V USB-C charger and cable. The LED display shows charging status (e.g., a gentle pulsing pattern while charging and a steady ring when full on most units). Actual time depends on battery capacity and adapter output. High-voltage fast-charge modes are not recommended.
Safety — is this a medical device?
No. bitebuddy provides educational feedback about eating progress and nutrition. It is not intended to diagnose, treat, or prevent disease.
